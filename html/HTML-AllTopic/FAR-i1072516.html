46.103 Contracting office responsibilities.

Contracting offices are responsible for-

  1. (a) Receiving from the activity responsible for technical requirements any specifications for inspection, testing, and other contract quality requirements essential to ensure the integrity of the supplies or services (the activity responsible for technical requirements is responsible for prescribing contract quality requirements, such as inspection and testing requirements or, for service contracts, a quality assurance surveillance plan);

  2. (b) Including in solicitations and contracts the appropriate requirements for the contractorâ€™s control of quality for the supplies or services to be acquired;

  3. (c) Issuing any necessary instructions to the cognizant contract administration office and acting on recommendations submitted by that office (see 42.301 and 46.104(f));

  4. (d) When contract administration is retained (see 42.201), verifying that the contractor fulfills the contract quality requirements; and

  5. (e) Ensuring that nonconformances are identified, and establishing the significance of a nonconformance when considering the acceptability of supplies or services which do not meet contract requirements.
