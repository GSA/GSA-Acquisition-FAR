52.223-15 Energy Efficiency in Energy-Consuming Products.

Energy Efficiency in Energy-Consuming Products (May 2020)

  1. (a) Definition. As used in this clause—

    Energy-efficient product

    1. (1) Means a product that–

      1. (i) Meets Department of Energy and Environmental Protection Agency criteria for use of the Energy Star trademark label; or

      2. (ii) Is in the upper 25 percent of efficiency for all similar products as designated by the Department of Energy’s Federal Energy Management Program.

    2. (2) The term “product” does not include any energy-consuming product or system designed or procured for combat or combat-related missions (42 U.S.C. 8259b).

  2. (b) The Contractor shall ensure that energy-consuming products are energy efficient products (i.e., ENERGY STAR® products or FEMP-designated products) at the time of contract award, for products that are—

    1. (1) Delivered;

    2. (2) Acquired by the Contractor for use in performing services at a Federally-controlled facility;

    3. (3) Furnished by the Contractor for use by the Government; or

    4. (4) Specified in the design of a building or work, or incorporated during its construction, renovation, or maintenance.

  3. (c) The requirements of paragraph (b) apply to the Contractor (including any subcontractor) unless—

    1. (1) The energy-consuming product is not listed in the ENERGY STAR® Program or FEMP; or

    2. (2) Otherwise approved in writing by the Contracting Officer.

  4. (d) Information about these products is available for—

    1. (1) ENERGY STAR® at http://www.energystar.gov/products; and

    2. (2) FEMP at https://www.energy.gov/eere/femp/energy-efficient-products-and-energy-saving-technologies.

(End of clause)
