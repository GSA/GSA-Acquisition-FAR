50.205-4 Authorization of awards made presuming SAFETY Act designation or certification after contract award.

  1. (a) When necessary to award a contract prior to DHS issuing SAFETY Act protections, contracting officers may award contracts presuming that DHS will issue a SAFETY Act designation/certification to the contractor after contract award only if-

    1. (1) The criteria of 50.205-3(a) are met;

    2. (2) The chief of the contracting office (or other official designated in agency procedures) approves the action; and

    3. (3) The contracting officer advises DHS of the timelines for potential award and consults DHS as to when DHS could reasonably complete evaluations of offerorsâ€™ applications for SAFETY Act designations or certifications.

  2. (b) Contracting officers shall not authorize offers presuming that SAFETY Act certification will be obtained (as opposed to a SAFETY Act designation), unless a block certification applies to the class of technology to be acquired under the solicitation.
